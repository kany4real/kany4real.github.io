Biography

Kany J. Henry

Congolese writer-director, screenwriter, editor and series creator based in Conakry.

Kany J. Henry has built his journey across the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Cameroon and Guinea. This trajectory feeds a pan-African perspective attentive to cities, movement, power structures and intimate choices made under collective pressure.

He approaches cinema through writing, directing and editing. His short films Et Si… ? and Le Bruit du Silence explore silence, family, tradition and the moral consequences of personal decisions. He also worked as co-director and co-writer on Les Dédales du Rêveur and Debout Malgré Tout.

His development slate now spans series and feature films. Through Sortie de Route, La Veine Bleue and Une Clé à Conakry, he develops genre stories rooted in contemporary African realities: supernatural thriller, moral drama, geopolitical tension and urban dramedy.

Trained in directing, series creation and feature screenplay writing, he thinks about mise-en-scène from the page onward. Rhythm, atmosphere, space, silence and point of view are part of his writing process. His ambition is to carry culturally precise stories to international audiences without erasing the territory they come from.

Based in Conakry, Kany J. Henry continues to develop his projects and seeks producers, co-producers, broadcasters and partners able to bring ambitious African stories to the screen.
