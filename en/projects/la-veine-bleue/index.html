She came to audit a mine. She discovers the invisible price of power.

La Veine Bleue

Logline

Between Abidjan and Katanga, an Ivorian lawyer sent to audit a mining group uncovers what its compliance reports conceal and must choose between the career she was promised and a truth capable of shaking the system that let her in.

Pitch

Amara Diop works in the rooms where appearances are negotiated: audits, compliance, responsibility, career. Sent to Katanga for an assignment meant to strengthen her position, she is confronted with what clean reports leave out. The documents align, the numbers reassure, the procedures seem to work, but every interview and every silence shifts the line between professional loyalty and complicity.

La Veine Bleue follows a woman who has learned how to move through structures of power and who discovers the moral cost of that ascent. A geopolitical thriller and moral drama, the series asks what remains of the self when a truth capable of shaking an entire system also threatens the place one fought to reach.

Narrative Promise

A moral thriller about ambition, responsibility and the opaque circuits of a global economy that begins far from public view.

World

The series connects boardrooms, legal archives, African cities and mining territories in a tension where every document can become evidence, weapon or sentence.

Themes

Power, resources, responsibility, social ascent, extraction, institutional silence, loyalty under pressure.

Public Character

  • Amara Diop: an Ivorian lawyer confronted with the invisible machinery of the system she serves.

Stage

Season bible developed.
