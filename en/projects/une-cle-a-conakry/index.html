Une Clé à Conakry

Logline

In Conakry, a young man from a Congolese family pretends to be engaged so he can rent a room of his own, only to discover that freedom costs more than rent.

Pitch

Henry Mupeta, 19, lives in a house full of women, watchful eyes and truths he would rather avoid. He wants a room of his own, a place where he can finally be treated as an adult. But in Conakry, finding housing without stable income, guarantees or social status takes more than a desire for independence.

To open a door, Henry lets people believe he is engaged. The lie works, then spills over. His family, the landlord, the young woman drawn into the arrangement and the surrounding neighbourhood are pulled into a warm urban family dramedy where shame, tenderness and dignity are negotiated every day. Une Clé à Conakry treats coming of age as something concrete: paying, answering for oneself, making repairs, telling the truth, and realizing that a key is not enough when the life it opens rests on a lie.

Narrative Promise

A warm urban family dramedy about independence, social shame and learning responsibility.

World

Conakry is not a backdrop: the city shapes relationships between family, neighbourhood, housing, work, reputation and a person’s word.

Themes

Family, autonomy, lies, dignity, youth, housing, fragile masculinity, solidarity, popular Conakry.

Public Characters

  • Henry Mupeta: a young man who wants to be taken seriously before he has learned how to keep his word.
  • Saran: a young woman drawn into an arrangement that forces her to defend her own word.

Stage

Developed screenplay.
